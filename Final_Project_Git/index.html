Crime in the United States

A Visual Exploration of Crime Data

Why Should We Care About Crime in the US?

Police brutality in the US has brought about one of the largest civil rights movements in recent history. To many, the solution is to 'defund the police' or allocate resources away from police and towards social services. Others believe this would leave Americans unprotected against violent crime. In this project I aim to explore crime by crime type, geography and it's relationship with police presence. I hope that this can serve as a resource for my peers engaging in debates about the effectiveness of our police forces.

The data for this analysis comes from the FBI Crime Data Tables. Data was pulled from the Summary Reporting System, Police Employee Data and Crime Data Explorer. Let's begin by examing the overall US crime rate over the past 10 years:

We can see clearly that the trend has generally been negative with a slight increase from 2014-2016. However overall crime is much lower than in past decades.
